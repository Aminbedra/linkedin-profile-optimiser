# Audit Rubric

Score each dimension below, then sum the weighted points into an overall score out of 100. Present the overall number first, then the per-dimension breakdown. Mark each dimension as Strong, Partial, or Weak with a one-line reason.

Some dimensions cannot be judged from text alone (photo quality, banner). When the source material does not reveal them, mark them "Not visible, confirm" rather than guessing.

## Dimensions and weights

| Dimension | Weight | What "Strong" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | 20 | Leads with value and target keywords, not just a job title. Speaks to who they help and how. |
| About / summary | 20 | Opens with a hook, establishes credibility, states what they do and for whom, includes proof, ends with a call to action. Written in first person. |
| Experience | 20 | Achievement-led bullets with quantified results, not duty lists. Most recent role is fullest. |
| Keyword discoverability | 15 | Target-role keywords appear in headline, About, skills, and job titles. |
| Skills | 10 | Relevant to the target role, ordered sensibly, not padded with irrelevant ones. |
| Featured / media | 5 | Showcases proof: posts, articles, portfolio, press, or case studies. |
| Recommendations | 5 | At least a few specific, recent recommendations. |
| Profile basics | 5 | Custom URL, professional photo, banner, location, and contact set. |

## Scoring guide per dimension

Convert each dimension's quality to a fraction of its weight:
- Strong = full weight
- Partial = roughly half weight
- Weak = zero to a quarter weight

Sum the awarded points for the overall score.

## Banding

- 80 to 100: Recruiter-ready. Polish only.
- 60 to 79: Solid base, clear high-impact fixes available.
- 40 to 59: Functional but underselling. Needs rewrites.
- Below 40: Significant gaps. Prioritise headline, About, and experience first.

## Common failure patterns to flag

- Headline is only a job title with no value or keywords.
- About section is third person or empty, or is a list of adjectives with no proof.
- Experience entries describe responsibilities ("Responsible for...") instead of outcomes.
- Skills section is generic or unrelated to the stated target.
- No quantified results anywhere.
- Keywords for the target role appear nowhere searchable.
