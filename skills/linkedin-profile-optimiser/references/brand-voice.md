# Personal Brand Voice and Content Angle

This is the optional growth layer. A clean profile gets you found; a clear brand makes you remembered. Frame it that way to the user.

## Positioning angle

Help the user finish one sentence: "I am the person who helps [audience] [achieve outcome], unlike others because [differentiator]."

A strong angle is specific and ownable. "Marketing expert" is forgettable. "I help Nordic SaaS founders win their first ten UK customers" is a position someone remembers and refers.

Probe for the differentiator: a niche, a method, a contrarian view, a combination of skills, or a lived background. The differentiator is the hook the rest hangs on.

## Content pillars

Pick two or three themes the user can post about repeatedly without running dry. Good pillars sit where their expertise, their audience's questions, and their differentiator overlap.

For each pillar, suggest three example post angles so the user has an immediate starting point. Keep examples concrete, not "share tips about marketing."

## Voice

Match the user's natural register rather than imposing a generic LinkedIn tone. Note two or three voice traits to keep consistent (for example: plain-spoken, evidence-led, lightly personal). Consistency of voice is what makes a feed feel like a person rather than a brochure.

## Output

A one-line positioning statement, two or three named pillars each with three post angles, and a short voice note. Keep it to a page. This is a springboard, not a content calendar.
